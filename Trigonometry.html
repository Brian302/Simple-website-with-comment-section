Trigonometry

This topic is known for the many confusing and complicated trig identities, but if you get the foundations down you will be able to understand how to solve related problems. In theory, trigonometry the branch of mathematics concerned with specific functions of angles and their application to calculations. There's not a lot of very interesting facts but I will connect this topic with the topics that I talked about before.

  1. Famous Trigonometric Identity

    2. One of the most famous identities is definitely the sum of sin2θ and cos2θ. To the untrained eye, it seems fascinating that the sum of two trig functions equals 1. But if you incorporate the Pythagorean Theorum, it will become easy to understand.

    First, you can rewrite a,b using the sine function to get two new equations. Next, we can plug in those two variables a,b into the Pythagorean Theorum. Finally, we will get our solution to the proof after simplying the equation. Easy isn't it!

  3. The Unit Circle

    4. The unit circle is a useful tool to better visualize the relations between different trig functions. In simple words, it is a circle with radius 1 in the Cartesian plane where the center is at (0,0). Firstly, it is another visual proof for the Pythagorean Theorum.
